V.E.R.G.

In UF's Virtual Experience Research Group, we simulate personalized virtual reality experiences to train medical professionals and the police force through human-computer interaction. Currently, Daniel Sepler and I have paired up with the UF Police Department and the Crisis Intervention Training Center to reenact role play situations. In this situation, as cop the cop you receive a call from dispatch from a pharmacist. You then walk in to CVS and must interact, empathize, and calm down a bipolar women who is having a panic attack because she is unable to receive more medication.

The experience will be presented and used by the UFPD. We have conducted psychological research to conduct the experience and are using Unity 3D and Maya. We have presented our beta and have gotten positive feedback. With a new grant, are now working on our Alpha to present at the CIT International Annual Conference.

Facebook Travel

I have a passion for traveling and exploring the world. As a new project, I want to create a plug-in for Facebook to connect friends and travel. Have you seen a friend that has taken a trip that you think you would have enjoyed? Do you know someone who lived somewhere that you are visiting and want to know what places they would suggest?

Using location APIs and the user's permission, you can share with your friends where you suggest without having to track it your self and spark this sense of social traveling.

Gator VR

The VR / AR revolution is booming. With a computer, communications, and engineering background, and a passion for combining these skills to create magic, I was quickly introduced to various VR projects. Through ACM, I decided to start building a VR community at UF to bring students, teachers, and researchers from different fields interested in VR development together. We are promoting the research and development of virtual and augmented reality applications and will be starting Fall 2016. With our recent Cry Engine lab grant, we want to arm students with the tools and resources they need to pursue the future of VR computing and work with this cutting-edge technology.

We will connect students with professors and industry leaders, hold workshops and build project teams to give students first-hand exposure to VR and AR, yet most importantly create a VR community here at UF for future collaboration and interests.

Let's shape the Digital Future. I am always up for chatting or collaborating with beautiful minds so feel free to contact me!
