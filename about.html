V.E.R.G.

In UF's Virtual Experience Research Group, we simulate personalized virtual reality experiences to train medical professionals and newly the police force through human-computer interaction. Currently, Daniel Sepler and I have paired up with the UF Police Department to reenact a role play situation of a cop that has received a call from a pharmacist. As the cop, you walk in to CVS and must interact and then calm down a bipolar women who is having a panic attack because she is unable to receive more medication.

The experience will be presented and used by the UFPD. We have conducted psychological research to conduct the experience and are using Unity 3D and Maya.

Paper Dreams

As our Senior Project, Kendall Robertson and I are creating an interactive virtual reality music video. It will be set to the song, Paper Dreams, a recently recorded single from local Gainesville artist Ysatis Hernandez and her accompaniment rapper Cliff. The video allows users to “look around” at the world around them in real"time as the music plays using VR headsets like Google Cardboard and the Oculus Rift, or simply the arrow keys on the video. Rather than using only real life videography, our goal is to create a virtual “fantasy world” where we will use multiple digital design platforms to model, map, animate, and composite this virtual “world”.

We are using the After Effects plug-in Skybox Studio by Mettle which allows us to create animated environment maps to be used with 3D models. We will also be using the Element 3D plug-in and the DomeMaster 3D plug-in in Maya. The video will be published at the end of April. Stay tuned!
